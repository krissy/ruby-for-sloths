Introduction to Coding Concepts using Ruby (and sloths).

Hellooooo, worlddddd.

This workshop is given by software engineer Kriselda Rabino for AcornHack:Girls 2016, a unique hackathon involving young female entrepreneurs aged 12-19 in celebration of International Women's Day (in partnership with Ada's List, Girls In Tech, GeekGirls Meetup, Geekettes, Blooming Founders and UnLtd).

It introduces basic coding concepts using Ruby, and has a few too many sloth analogies in it due to Kris' unhealthy obsession with these slow, amusing creatures.

What we'll cover:

  1. So, who's this Ruby kid anyway?
  2. What's a program?
  3. Playing with Ruby.
  4. Mistakes are inevitable; accept them, embrace them. (aka Errors are your new BFF)
  5. What does 'Object-Oriented' mean? How to think in things and classes.
  6. Ruby's basic data types: numbers and strings and arrays, oh my!
  7. Methods and madness.
  8. Variables = "super handy"
  9. Decisions, decisions. Conditional Statements are here to help.
  10. Iterating over collections (now it's getting a bit loopy)
  11. Your turn! Let's make some code.
